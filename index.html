NYC JOBS

Data Science Project

Overview:

This project looks at the current number of job postings that are currently available on the City of New Yorkâ€™s official jobs site.

With the use of the packages like pandas,numpy, and other tools this project makes an in-depth analysis on the current number of job posting on the official job site.

Data:

There was only one dataset that was used for this project and it can be found over on the site: https://data.cityofnewyork.us/City-Government/NYC-Jobs/kpav-sd4t/data

This dataset contains all the internal and external postings on the job site. It contains the salary range, name of agencies, posting type, job categories, work locations etc. The underlying assumption is that the most popular job in NYC will be in the sector of engineering.

Analysis:

Upon analysing the dataset, it was seen that there were a total of 54 unique agencies that are actively posting on the website. It was also noticed that there are over 100 job categories that have been posted so far.

Let us take a look at the most posted Job Category on the website. On the right it can be seen that in the category of Technology has the highest presence in the postings with Data Innovation right behind it, and Research Analysis and Architecture in third and fourth respectively.

Now let us take a look at the most posted part-time jobs by a certain agency. On the right it can be seen that the health hectors have the highest number of part time positions open with the department of health posting the highet number of part time jobs.

Now let us take a look at the most posted full-time jobs by a certain agency. On the right it can be seen that the department of environment protection has the highest number of full time jobs posted with an astonishing number of 302 postings.

Lastly, a comparison was done about how much was being offered by the top 5 job categories that have been posted by the agencies. With the help of pandas, the mean of the top 5 job categories were calculated and plotted as a bar graph.From the graph it can be seen that most salaries are in the high sixties and low seventies.

Conclusion:

The assumption that engineering would be the most popular in NYC was wrong. However, technology being the highest posted category goes to how the demand of that sector. In addition to that, that sector also provide competitieve salaries and it something everyone should be keeping their eyes on when they are deciding what their career.
